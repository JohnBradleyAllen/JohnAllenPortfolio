About Me

I started my professional career in the United States Marine Corps. During my time in the Marines, I served as a combat engineer responsible for detecting, identifying, and the reduction of improvised explosive devices and their components. My first deployment was right outside of Ar-Rutbah, Iraq with the 3rd Battalion, 9th Marines.
I deployed for a second time to Marjah, Afghanistan with the 3rd Battalion, 6th Marines.
I earned the rank of Sergeant before honorably separating.

I then joined the Delaware Air National Guard where I attended the Louis F. Garland Fire Academy and graduated at the top of my class. As a Department of Defense firefighter, I was responsible for the initial response capability to medical and fire emergencies for all the aircraft, buildings, and personnel on the installation.
Along with being a skilled firefighter, I was a Nationally Registered Emergency Medical Technician.
I honorably discharged at the rank of Staff Sergeant.

I completed a bachelor of science in computer science from Wilmington University. At that time I was a Satellite Communications Administrator contractor in support of Blue Force Trackers worldwide. I gained experience in basic Linux during this time. I then became a system administrator contractor for the United States Army enterprise network. I gained experience in system hardening through a defense-in-depth approach. I am well versed with Assured Compliance Assessment Solution, a vulnerability scanner used for vulnerability scanning and risk assessment by agencies of the United States Department of Defense. I have experience in system hardening DOD systems through the implementation of Security Technical Implementation Guides that enhance security for software, hardware, physical and logical architectures to further reduce vulnerabilities.

Beyond my professional technical experience, I am very interested and passionate about offensive security. You could say I am an aspiring penetration tester. I believe that constantly probing targets from an ethical standard is the most efficient way in finding vulnerabilities before criminal hackers do. I spend a large portion of my leisure time doing penetration testing training on my home lab with content from INE while listening to Darknet Diaries.
